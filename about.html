Palm Jaggery Making Process

   A palm tree climber who collects Padani or palm juice from palm tree, everyday to make Karuppatti (palm jaggery). Palm jaggery making is a small-scale industry and is found in plenty in the Tuticorin and Tirunelveli districts in Tamilnadu.

   The collected Palm juice or padani from the palm trees is filtered and boiled in a huge vessel for more than 2 hours. The syrup gets thicker and viscous. Under the peak sun, in a hut made of palm leaves, Palm jaggary maker spends hours stirring this syrup constantly. She removes the boiling syrup at a point where the syrup when cooled in water becomes a firm caramel ball. Now The syrup is ready to pour into coconut shells to set. It’s color was almost a pale yellow as opposed to the black ones you get in the market. The darker ones are those with additives like colour, flour and sugar that you find in the market. The leftover syrup in the vessel that was collected by a tool, it is look like a juicy ball. It tastes really nice like juicy and caramel candy.

Palm Jaggery Uses in Recipe:

   1. In sweets, beverages, and curries, use this jaggery like you would sugar or cane jaggery.

   2. Crush the jaggery with your fingers before adding it to the sauce if you're using it in a curry.

   3. Liquid Palm Jaggery is delicious ice cream and toast topping or syrup. It goes well with Rotis and bhakris as well.

   4. If you're creating a dish that calls for sugar syrup, you may easily substitute liquid jaggery for the sugar.

   5. If you're using this jaggery in a hot milk beverage, wait until it's cool before adding it. The beverage will not split this way.

Palm Jaggery v/s Jaggery:

   The first difference between both is, that Karupatti is derived from the palm trees whereas Jaggery is obtained from the sugarcane. Normal jaggery is enjoyed in its crystalised form, having been prepared by boiling, churning, and sifting it using age-old processes. At the same time, karupatti is manufactured by extracting palm sap, which is then cooked and churned by hand.

Tips to Store Palm Jaggery:

Liquid Palm Jaggery:

   Keep liquid palm jaggery in an airtight container at all times. This jaggery should be stored in the refrigerator once opened. To remove the jaggery from the jar, always use a clean, dry spoon.

Palm Jaggery Blocks:

   We'll advise keeping blocks of Palm Jaggery in air tight sealed containers. Contact with moisture and air should be kept to a bare minimum. The jaggery is easy to crush. Even your fingers would be able to smash medium-sized bits of this jaggery. You can replace a piece of standard cane jaggery with palm jaggery.
